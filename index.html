Call for Papers

Secure, fair, and privacy-preserving optimization and learning

Modern computational intelligence applications increasingly operate in distributed environments where data is scattered across multiple edge devices and organizations. This distributed paradigm offers unprecedented computational power and data diversity. However, it also introduces critical challenges in security, privacy protection, and fairness—issues that traditional optimization and learning methods were not designed to address. Growing concerns about data security, regulatory requirements (e.g., GDPR, CCPA), and stakeholder equity call for a fundamental rethinking of optimization and learning approaches. Developing new secure, privacy-preserving, and fairness-aware optimization and learning paradigms is essential to leverage distributed computing and storage while safeguarding sensitive information, preventing malicious attacks, and ensuring equitable outcomes for all participants.

In recent years, federated learning has emerged as a popular machine learning paradigm that enables the use of distributed data without leaking sensitive information. Federated learning builds global models by aggregating local models trained separately on different devices using local data. Yet, security and privacy-preserving optimization have received considerably less attention, particularly for evolutionary and data-driven approaches commonly used in many real-world optimization scenarios. Furthermore, data-driven optimization can be affected by data or algorithmic bias, producing unfair results with serious consequences. When objective function values correlate with tangible rewards (e.g., financial gains, resource allocation, or career opportunities), stakeholders may hesitate to participate in collaborative optimization if benefits are disproportionately distributed or if the system is vulnerable to adversarial manipulation. These factors present unique security and fairness challenges distinct from those in classification or prediction tasks, highlighting the need for secure, privacy-preserving, and fairness-aware optimization techniques to build trustworthy and sustainable collaborative ecosystems.

This special session aims to bring together researchers and practitioners to advance the state-of-the-art in secure, fair, and privacy-preserving optimization and learning. We seek to foster intradisciplinary collaboration across evolutionary computation, neural networks, and fuzzy systems communities to develop novel algorithms that simultaneously optimize performance, ensure security against adversarial threats, protect privacy, and guarantee fairness. The session will provide a platform to discuss theoretical foundations, algorithmic innovations, practical implementations, evaluation methods, and real-world applications of these paradigms.

Who should submit?

We welcome contributions from researchers and practitioners working on optimization, machine learning, federated and distributed systems, security, privacy, fairness, and multi-agent systems—from theory and methods to real-world applications.

Security-focused

Biographies

Xilu Wang

Xilu Wang is currently a Surrey Future Fellow, Bielefeld University, Germany. She obtained her Ph.D. in 2022, supervised by Prof. Yaochu Jin, with a thesis on “Bayesian Evolutionary Optimization for Heterogeneously Expensive Multi-objective Problems”. She was a Postdoctoral Fellow with Bielefeld University, Germany, from 2022 to 2024. She has published more than 36 papers in international journals and conferences. Her current research lines are Bayesian optimization, federated optimization, large language models and computational intelligence.

Dr. Lavanya Elluri

Dr. Lavanya Elluri is an Assistant Professor of Computer Information Systems at the Texas A&M University–Central Texas. She received a Ph.D. in Information Systems from the University of Maryland Baltimore County. She has worked for over a decade in the IT industry at reputed companies Infosys and REI Systems. She has led several projects at REI Systems and has extensive work experience with various databases and data warehousing technologies. She also has experience working on a wide range of data science and data analytics projects. Her research interests include Cybersecurity, Internet of Things, Semantic Web, Data privacy and Security, Text Mining, and Healthcare IT systems. Her research publications appeared in reputed venues such as Computers & Security, IEEE Big Data, IEEE Cloud, IEEE Access, and Journal of Computer Information Systems. She is the principal investigator on the National Science Foundation (NSF) Research grant.

Dr. Rajesh Murthy

Dr. Rajesh Murthy brings over three decades of ICT experience specializing in engineering secure and resilient software systems. His expertise encompasses integrating emergent technologies to drive business success and leading large-scale software systems evolution. Dr. Murthy's professional strengths lie in cross-functional collaboration, delivering robust solutions for complex computational challenges, and addressing modernization requirements in dynamic technological environments. His research interests focus on the intersection of software security, distributed systems architecture, and the practical implementation of privacy-preserving technologies in enterprise settings.
